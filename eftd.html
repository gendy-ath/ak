Where Land Meets Sea
The Greek islands neighbouring the Turkish coast have become the unofficial 'gate' for refugees trying to cross into Citadel Europe over the past year. Lesvos is perhaps the largest 'island' on the archipelago of the 'migrant corridor', along which physical and social space are set under constant negotiation. The rugged, mountainous landscape of the north is perforated by synthetic piles of survival, ones that challenge its legitimacy over the land. Curved is also the social terrain, with displaced people, locals and volunteers renegotiating social space in the island. Lesvos island, the shoreline. Where land meets sea.
